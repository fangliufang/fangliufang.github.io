李筱珊同学在中欧法学院2010年春季硕士生毕业典礼的发言（2010年3月13日）
发布于  2010/3/19 15:12:28
A deep breath and good morning.

Professor Colneric, Ambassador Abou, Mr. Zhang Fusen, our dear founding fathers, please allow me and Professor Huang Jin and Professor Fang Liufang, dear friends, dear family and the faculty of CESL,

First of all, I would like to thank you all for coming. On Tuesday when I was first told that I was the first student to give speech today, I wondered why? Why it's me and why I'm the first? At first I thought because in Chinese way it's usually the top student or the monitor who should give the speech, and then I think we have 43 Chinese students and only one European. So maybe it's like in the European Commission, the vast majority has the final say. [Laughter] And then it's not until yesterday I searched online that I found it's in United States, it has a tradition on the graduation ceremony, the top one student will give the farewell speech; but before her, the second highest ranked student will give the welcome speech. And so today I am here. [Laughter and applause] Thank you. And see this is CESL. This happens everyday in CESL. It's a place where the different cultures are blended and fused into each other, and the most fantascinating [fascinating] part charm that enchanted me most. And return to our topic. Today since my duty is to welcome you for your attendance.

First of all, I would do it, like it in a Chinese way to express my sincere gratitutes to all of you. First, I would like to thank our two founding fathers and CESL administrative staff members, and the flying faculty and especially the co-deans. Thank you very much for providing us a platform where we know ourselves better. Now you may think it’s like a cliché, but here in the next five minutes, I will seize this opportunity and presenting some ideas that I have longed for sharing with you.

Honestly speaking, seventeen months ago at the opening of the CESL, I supposed to graduate because I am one of the ten 2006 students. I supposed to find a job and earn money. Why I did start to apply CESL? A lot of friends asked me "Why? What do you want from CESL? It's just a glorious political project. And the knowledge of EU political design wont help you get a better job in China." To tell you the truth, I agreed with them, at that moment. The only motivation applyed is because it offers me a free trip to Europe. [Laughter and applause] Since I was in high school, the first destination of my traveling dream is Europe. I did a calculation: the expenses to Europe and second what like how much I can earn in the next 17months. And finally I concluded that go to the CESL is the most economic and the fastest way to achieve my dream. And plus a foreign diploma is far more that cannot be measured by money. So I finally come here.

But as time goes by, from you and from every details, I see CESL differently. It's dozens of times, our two founding fathers drove a long distance from center to Changping, thank you very much. And it's a personal invitation card Ambassador Abou sent to us. And it is Professor Fang's effort and patience with CUPL to solve the problems in our studied but also in our life. It's dean Colneric's hospitality and encouragement and integrity and your deep devotion to CESL, and you are always our spiritual support. And to our flying faculties, it's your patience on law and interests in China legal regime. And it's administrative staff, your efforts, your patience and your hard work make CESL run smoothly and assured the high quality of CESL. And I asked myself, these people are so passionate, so serious and have so many confidence in CESL.

Me, as the bigoted [biggest] direct beneficiary, why should be serious? To myself, why shouldn't I be responsible for my life, or at least to this seventeen months. So at the time I determined to maximize CESL resources, then I bought a new MP3 and do the recording for the every professor's lecture and try to understand every sentence of each professor. As I learned more as more comprehended, the more interests grow in EU. I started to appreciate and admire the well functioning of the EU institutions. I witness the magic power of law and trust through which the contradictions and divergence among 27 member strates [states] were coordinated. The immense prosperity and lasting peace have been realized. Today, if you ask me, "What do you want to do after graduation?"My answer maybe still be the same, "I'm not sure." But what makes today different, what makes me grateful for all of you and what makes me changed I think it's not only a different way of thinking, but the belief to have dream and the belief on the rule of law. If rule of law and mutual trust can make Europe such a prosperous and unified continent, why not in China?

And my dear classmates, I am proud to be the first generation of CESL graduates. As a self-organized group, we too sound eight CESL functions very well. Voluntarily and naturally, we have our computer-man, Zhang Xiaotao, helps to fix computer for every lecturer. And we have Song Defeng prepare the coffee and tea during every break. And also our dear friends in the city center, thank you very much for your patience and kindly help. For those who living in the Changping, life is much easier and more convenient. And also Sophia, Hui Miao, Zhang Jie, Elton, Cao Chun, Zhu Yong, a lot of you have devoted so much of your spare time on public service.

Under tight schedules, we have little spare time to hang out together, but I am 100% sure that the feeling of brotherhood and sisterhood was spreaded among us. Although heavy courses, tough exams, papers, every happy moment we share together in Europe-Day Party, along the Ulster lake, in Brussels, on Jundu Mountain, or in classroom 305 and even in Badaling Express Way, we will be accumulated and turned into most precious and extraordinary experience in our life, which is unique and can be never duplicated or replace. It's you, my dear friends, the great class 2008 who have given me what's most the memorable and valuable moment that I will take away from CESL, and I wish you all the best of luck and greatest future.

Last but not least, I would like to thank my parents and all those other parents who are not present today. You are the heroes. Without your understanding, passion and sacrifice, we could not make it today; and thank you for your selfless love and support both spiritually and financially. Thank you very much for respecting us and allowing us act in our own way.

Thank you.
