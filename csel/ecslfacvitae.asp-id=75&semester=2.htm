Personalia

Professional Address:  Institute of Environmental and Energy Law, Collegium Falconis, Katholieke Universiteit Leuven, Tiensestraat 41, PB 3414, B 3000 Leuven

Profession

Professor since 2002 at Law Faculty, K.U.Leuven and Member of the Brussels Bar

Education

Geert van Calster graduated from the Law Faculty of Leuven University in June 1993 (LL.B. ‘90, LL.M. ‘93) and obtained a Master’s degree (LL.M.) at the College of Europe, Bruges, in July 1994. 

In September 1994, he was granted a fellowship at the University of Leuven, first at the Institute of Administrative Law, as of 1995 at the Institute of Environmental and Energy Law (IMER). 

From September 1997 until September 1998, he was a Chevening Scholar at St. Edmund Hall, Oxford University.

He obtained a Ph.D. degree at Leuven University in May 1999, on the conflicts between international trade law and environmental protection. 

Other Professional Activities

At the Institute of Environmental and Energy Law, which he co-directs since June 2004, he teaches and researches European and International environmental law, European and International energy law, European economic law, and international trade law.  Between 2002 and 2004 he was also a visiting professor at Erasmus Universiteit, Rotterdam (Netherlands), where he held the chair of international economic law.  He is still involved in teaching at a variety of Universities in Europe, mainly on subjects of EC law, and was a visiting professor of EU law at Monash University, Melbourne, winter term 2007.

He was called to the Brussels Bar in February 1999, having worked as of counsel with the London and Brussels office of City law firm SJ Berwin & Co since 1995.  He is now of counsel (practising) in the DLA Piper Brussels office.  His practice focuses on EC economic law, including competition law, EC environmental law, and international trade law.  He is cited in the Legal 500 as being
“well regarded for the interface between economic and regulatory law”, and as having an
“excellent academic background aligned to pragmatic experience in both public and private sectors.” 
According to the Legal 500, clients refer to the level of service of van Calster’s team at DLA Piper as “outstanding”.

Geert’s expertise covers EC environmental law and EC economic law, EC and international energy law, international trade law; and international environmental law, subjects on which he publishes and speaks extensively. 

His most current research interests include the regulation of new technologies (nanotechnologies in particular), and climate change law.  His editorial works includes Member of the Editorial Board, Carbon & Climate Law Review; Member of the Editorial Board, Nanotechnology, Law and Business; and Associate editor, Law, Probability and Risk.

Prof van Calster directs the Master programme on Energy and Environmental law at the K.U.Leuven: http://www.law.kuleuven.be/imer/master, is a chair/research associate of the Research Fund, K.U.Leuven (2003-2013), and was a visiting lecturer at Oxford University (Sept 2006 ?C Sept 2008).
