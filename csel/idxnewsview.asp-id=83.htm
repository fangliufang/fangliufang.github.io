汉堡大学校长莫妮卡・库茨女士在中欧法学院成立庆典上致辞
发布于  2008/11/3 16:24:03
Dear Vice-Premier Li Keqiang,
Dear President Barroso,
Dear President Xu,
It is a pleasure and a privilege for me to be here in Beijing to address you at this very special event, the Opening Ceremony of the China-EU School of Law. I would like to convey our thanks to the European Commission and the Chinese government for their continual support of the China-EU School of Law. Our further gratitude is owed to the representatives of the European partner universities and associates for their tireless efforts and long-standing commitment to this project, and also for making this long trip to Beijing in order to attend this ceremony. I would also like to thank our Chinese partners, the Tsinghua University and the Chinese Prosecutors College, and, in particular, the China University of Political Science and Law, who have been instrumental in their endeavours to organise this ceremony and realise this project.

Without the enterprise and never-wavering support of all these persons involved, this ambitious project would not have been possible.

What makes the China-EU School of Law so unique is the high level of cooperation between the European and Chinese higher education institutions, not only with respect to the activities organised and implemented by the CESL, but also in terms of the structure of this project. In today's world of globalisation, open markets and technological innovations, international communication and cooperation are no longer abstract concepts, but rather they have grown to be the cornerstones in the foundations of the international community. Thus, it becomes more and more important to strengthen relations between Europe and other leading global players, particularly China. With China's growing role on the international stage and her strong position in world markets, communication and cooperation become necessary tools for establishing new projects, forging new bonds and strengthening existing relations. It is vital that higher education not only incorporates this stance, but also commits itself to setting the bar higher and increasing these notions. The role universities play in this process must be not only to impart factual knowledge to students, but also to impress upon them a way of thinking which reflects this philosophy-- the recognition that dialogue is to be encouraged, yet can only be fruitful when based on mutual respect; and that a desire to become thoroughly familiarised with other legal systems, economic structures, cultures and schools of thought is always beneficial for both parties, and is never a one-sided affair. It is only through the mutual exchange of knowledge, know-how and expertise that this goal can be achieved. The China-EU School of Law, by bringing together outstanding European and Chinese higher education institutions with high-ranking European and Chinese law firms, public organisations, and associated academic institutions, permeates all sectors of the legal system and provides for a comprehensive platform on which international legal dialogue can be held. To make such a large consortium function efficiently requires creativity, diligence, the willingness to adapt to new circumstances and to face new challenges, as well as vision and ingenuity. All these qualities characterise the China-EU School of Law.

The unrivalled position of the China-EU School of Law in the global sphere of higher education is not only provided for thanks to its strong relationship with China. It is also, from a cooperative point a view, a unique project within the European higher education sphere. The collaboration of thirteen European universities from all corners of the continent, not to mention the over twenty-seven European associates and sponsors who enrich the law school with their particular expertise, marks an unprecedented effort to consolidate the knowledge and know-how of the best legal scholars and practitioners Europe has to offer. It combines the European vision of communication and cooperation at an educational level with its vision on communication and cooperation at the legal practice level. This task is a mighty one, and can only be accomplished through constructive dialogue and an appreciation of the cultural and institutional differences which exist amongst universities from the different EU Member States, as well as the differences which exist between Europe and China. However, it is the very purpose of the China-EU School of Law to minimise such differences by promoting a greater understanding not only of each others legal systems, but also of each others culture and history.

Last but not least I would like congratulate all the students who are here today on your choice to enrol in the CESL's Master Programme. In this ambitious programme you will be taught by some of the best legal scholars from Europe and China, which will undoubtedly be a challenging, yet also highly beneficial learning experience for your future careers. By partaking in a programme that places a strong emphasis on a comparative, intercultural and also interdisciplinary approach to European law, you are being well-prepared to embark upon a path to becoming not only exceptional lawyers, but also global legal counsellors. You will not only be expanding your own legal background, but by default are also presented with the opportunity to participate in the international legal dialogue the China-EU School of Law has dedicated itself to. I trust you will find the programme stimulating and insightful-- not only academically, but also as an enriching cultural experience. I therefore wish you all possible success for your future time here in Beijing.
