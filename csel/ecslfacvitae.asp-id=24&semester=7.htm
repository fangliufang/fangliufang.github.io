Overview

Gary Lynch-Wood is a Lecturer in Law and Regulation at the University of Manchester, School of Law. He is the Course Director for undergraduate courses on Legal Methods and Systems and Environmental Law and postgraduate courses on Regulation and Corporate Social Responsibility and Environmental Responsibility and the Regulation of Business. Gary is the Co-Director of the Sustainability, Policy and Regulation Research Centre (SPaRRC), an externally funded centre that focuses on empirical research into regulation, social and environmental responsibility and corporate issues. His research mainly focuses on regulation and the social and environmental behaviour of firms, and he has published widely on these issues. Gary is also an Honorary Fellow of Gray’s Inn and a member of the China-EU School of Law (CESL).
