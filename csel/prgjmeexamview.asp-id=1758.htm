2011-2012学年欧洲-国际法第九单元补考试题
发布于  2012/6/1 15:57:53
第九单元补考试题：“国际经济法” (International Economic Law)

Title of Course: WTO Law
Name of Lecturer: Dr. Denise Prévost
Instructions:

This resit exam consists of 3 questions, two theoretical questions and one case study (problem) question. All three questions have to be answered.
However, in Questions 1 and 2 students must choose only ONE (1) out of the two (2) sub-questions (a)or (b) to answer.
Question 3 must be answered in full.
You may use any of the WTO Agreements during the exam.


Question 1:
Short Questions on Institutional Aspects of the WTO

Give brief but reasoned answers to any one of the two sub-questions listed below. Always support your answer with legal arguments and references to the relevant articles of WTO agreements, WTO practice and case law where appropriate.

a) Concerns have been raised with regard to the influence of developing country Members in WTO decision-making. Briefly explain these concerns, referring to both the rules and the practice on decision-making in the WTO.
b) What rules of interpretation must a WTO panel apply when interpreting a provision in a WTO agreement? Briefly set out these rules and indicate on what basis you think they are the applicable rules for purposes of interpretation in WTO dispute settlement.


Question 2:
Short Questions on Substantive Aspects of WTO Law

Give brief but reasoned answers to any one of the two sub-questions listed below. Always support your answer with legal arguments and references to the relevant articles of WTO agreements, WTO practice and case law where appropriate.

(a) Discuss briefly how the likeness of products is determined for purposes of Article I:1 of the GATT. In your answer, indicate whether differences in the way in which products are produced (e.g. an environmentally friendly versus a polluting production method, or production involving child labour versus production not involving child labour) are relevant to the determination of the likeness under Article I:1 of the GATT.
(b) Are measures affecting trade in police services subject to a most favoured nation treatment obligation under the GATS? In your answer, explain the scope of application of the GATS Agreement.


Question 3:
Case study question

Farland, a WTO Member, is severely affected by the global economic crisis. To boost the national economy and halt the increase in unemployment, Farland has adopted a huge economic stimulus programme. To finance this programme, Farland has decided to take the measures set out below.

Farland has increased the customs duties on certain tobacco products. In Farland’s Schedule of Concessions, all tobacco products are subject to the tariff binding of 20% ad valorem, but Farland previously applied a customs duty of 7% ad valorem to these products. This customs duty has now been doubled with respect to cigarettes and cigars (now 14% ad valorem) and raised to 20% ad valorem for loose-leaf tobacco. In addition, Farland charges a fee of 5% ad valorem on all tobacco products for quality inspection services provided at the border.

Neighbouring Southland, is a significant producer of loose-leaf tobacco, but produces very limited quantities of cigars or cigarettes. Farland imports cigars and cigarettes primarily from the Republic of Bundastan, a WTO Member.

May Farland unilaterally increase its customs duties and impose fees on tobacco products in the way described above and remain consistent with its obligations under WTO law? Explain which provisions of WTO law are relevant in this respect and whether these provisions are complied with in the case at hand. Do not address the question whether possible violations may be justified under an exception in WTO law.
