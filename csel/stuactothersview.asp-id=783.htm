中欧法学院学生代表队从上海世博会发回的报道（2010年5月12日）
发布于  2010/9/9 10:10:13
2010年5月10日-11日，在中欧法学院院长秘书李怡茕带领下， 中欧法学院学生李博、李冉、何欣、吴京、陈婷婷、Valeria Poppa6人组成团队，参加了上海世博会的活动，以下为学生发回的英文报道：

CESL students took part in the Model EU, at the China Europe International Business School (CEIBS) from May 10th to 12th in Shanghai. Students gained experience of EU institutions and procedures, as well as a having a chance to practice their skills of negotiation and compromise.

Etienne Reuter, from the European Commission, Rolf D. Cremer, Dean and Vice President of CEIBS and CESL Co-dean Ninon Colneric gave opening speeches. Representatives from the European Parliament, the European Commission and the Council of Ministers attended.

CESL students, already tired from attending Europe Day celebrations the night before, had little time to rest. Day one commenced with a brief introduction. Students were assigned roles in the European Parliament, the Council of Ministers and as lobbyists. The idea was to debate proposed legislation limiting CO2 emissions from cars. Various options were put before participants including an integrated approach, where costs would be born by the whole industry or where the entire cost would be met by the car industry. Measurement of CO2 emissions, penalties and a timescale for implementation were also key factors in negotiations.

CESL student Li Bo, otherwise known as President of the Environment Committee, felt that his training at CESL helped him to think logically and form reasoned opinions. He felt that even though he had to act like a politician, his legal training made him more persuasive, as he would provide data and research to support his position. The time in Shanghai also gave him an opportunity to learn more about EU procedures and institutions, which will be helpful next year when he begins his European law studies.

Day two began with the announcement of the Commission’s draft proposals. The European Parliament and the Council of Ministers then began to establish their positions. Lobbyists also had a chance to try to exert their influence and win concessions, using both formal and informal strategies. CESL student Wu Jing, representative for Friends of the Earth, described her use of coffee breaks to try to convince member states. Even if the vested interests of larger states, such as France and Germany who have large automobile industries, often prevailed.

The Council of Ministers also listened to representations from member states. Third year law student He Xin played the part of Minister for Lithuania. He and representatives from other smaller states stood together in an attempt to force the larger powers to grant concessions. He managed to successfully advocate an increase in the level of carbon emissions, pursuant with Lithuania’s objective to reduce costs on citizens wishing to upgrade old soviet era cars.

A public hearing was held by the European Parliament, attended by the Commission, the Council and stakeholders, and a draft opinion was released. This gave CESL students further chance to demonstrate their skills and advocate for their respective countries and committees. Li Bo, however, felt that one key skill was that of compromise.

Ultimately, efforts to find a compromise failed and it proved impossible to reach a consensus among the Council of Ministers and the presidency in the time allowed and the outcome was a second reading. However, students still had the opportunity to share new experiences, meet new friends and interact with representatives from EU institutions. And as Pat Cox, former president of the European Parliament told students, the goal of law making in the EU is ‘harmony and diversity,’ even if this does take a little more time.


我院学生访问中欧工商管理学院 （左起：李博、李冉、吴京、何欣、Valeria Poppa、陈婷婷）
