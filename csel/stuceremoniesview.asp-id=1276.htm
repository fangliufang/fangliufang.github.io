韩菲娅同学在中欧法学院2010年春季硕士生毕业典礼的发言（2010年3月13日）
发布于  2010/3/19 15:14:00
Wo hui lai [I'm back] [Applause]


Dear Dean Colneric, dear Ambassador Abou, dear Mr. Zhang, dear Mr. Huang, dear Dean Fang, dear fellow students, dear friends, families, ladies and gentlemen,

Actually I cannot believe it myself that today I am standing here in front you all supposed to give a farewell speech after almost one and a half years full of studying, gathering experiences, and enjoying life in China.

But let me start at the beginning of this marvelous time of my life which definitely was one of the best I ever had. I came to China quite spontaneously, since I got to know the CESL more or less by accident. One and a half years ago, I only had three days to decide whether or not to quit my life in Germany and come to China, and not knowing what to expect here. As you can see, I dared to. Even though, I didn't know what to expect. When the taxi driver dropped me off the Changping Campus, you can imagine that I feel like a complete stranger. There were crowds of Chinese students around me, and everybody was looking at me. And I heard lots of them say, "Oh, ta zhende hao gao a!" [Laughter and applause] meaning "Oh, she's quite tall!" And suddenly I knew that for the next one and half years, I would have to expect such reactions.

Honestly, since I am back in Germany, I have something like attention deficit syndrome because nobody's looking at me anymore. [Laughter] But joking apart, I started a new life in China. I got to know my fellow students and I soon started to feel Chinese, in some way. Everybody of you will agree that we had a hard time doing our master programme. Almost every three days, we got a new professor, teaching a new subject, and everybody expecting us to work hard and make the best possible out of us.

Nevertheless, besides learning a lot, we could also enjoy a lot of interesting, even funny situations. And we all knew we had the best professors available in Europe. For me, as a German, it was especially interesting to get to know so many professors from different countries. In comparison to China, Europe is a quite small part of the world. But every country in Europe claims to be the most beautiful, to have a completely different cultures from the others, to have a different mentality and so on, and so on. And of course, Germans have a lot of jokes and prejudices towards the Italians, the Greek[s], the Spanish, almost everybody. Since I was confronted with so many different nationalities, I had the chance to check whether these prejudices are right or wrong. And as a result, I can say that it was really a personal gain to experience different points of view to see how people think and how they think about Germany especially, and to better understand what problems their countries and their inhabitants have to face.

As you all know, the program focuses on European Law, consequently we were talking about Europe, a lot. Especially worth mentioning from my perspective, is the fact that getting deeper insights into this ambitious project which is called European Union. And for the first time I really felt like a European. And I have to add in brackets, that's not only because I was the only European in class. No it was because I experienced that we have the same problems, that we have the same way of thinking and the same humour which unfortunately sometimes my Chinese colleagues didn't get, so it was always the situation that the professor and me were laughing a lot and they were asking me, "What did he say? Why are you laughing, I think it's not really funny?" But we have the same problem when you were telling me a joke and I am like, "Why are you laughing?" [Laughter] So that's quite interesting.

And I think that the Europeans all have the same ideal which is moving together due to the motto unification and diversity [United in Diversity], and for me that's really a very important experience. Besides gathering knowledge about the European Union, international and Chinese law, I particularly had a huge gain in personal experiences. When I came to Changping, my fellow students welcomed me very warm heartedly. Inspired by the lectures, we discussed a lot about several issues and problems and had the chance to explain and justify our points of view. Through those discussions, I learned a lot about the Chinese way of thinking and solving problems. For sure, to everybody of us, it is obvious how huge the differences of Chinese culture from the European culture are. But these huge differences are among other things the reason why the CESL was founded. The so-called culture-gaps caught my attention and I pursued the aim to better understand the Chinese people. With a view to this aim, I was provided with the best circumstances possible because I was the only one. And around me, there were just Chinese people. And so, I think my way of life was quite Chinese, (that's at least my friends always said). I had the chance to spend last year's spring festival at my best friend's home, and I was integrated into a Chinese family. And since that time I have a Chinese mam and dad. [Laughter] Xiexie nimen huanying wo zai lao hukou, wo zhende hen gaoxing renshi nimen [Thank you for welcoming me into your home, I am really happy to have got to know you] [Applause]

Whenever I had free time I took my chance to go traveling. After these one and a half years, I have seen a lot of places in China. I have been to places like Gobi dessert, to several monasteries and small villages in Gansu and Qinghai province. And I have seen people working in their rice fields in Yuanyang in Yunnan province. China has so many impressive cities and famous sites; but the sites which make me think were not these famous ones. My interest was especially caught by the small villages and the people spending their lives there. Talking to those people, who often didn't believe that they were talking to a foreigner, meant a lot to me. They were living in a completely different world. And when you listened to their problems, you suddenly feel ashamed, because you yourself are living in a world where such existential problems don't exist, but you are nevertheless complaining. These trips around China made me have a different view on my own life and my own situation and taught me to appreciate it more than I had done up to this point of time. I know for sure that I am a different person than I was before I came to China and I am so grateful that I had the chance to make all those experiences which I think from now on will shape my life and which I will never forget.

Dean Colneric, Dean Fang, I want to thank you as well on the behalf of my fellow students that you put so much effort into this project and tried to optimize it wherever possible. I hope that especially from the European side, students' interest will grow so that much more European students take the chance to participate, and thus tighten the relationship between China and Europe.

My last sentences are addressed to my fellow students. My dear friends, we spent quite a long time together, studying and discussing everyday, but as well having a lot of fun. I want to thank you for everything. You tried to give me an insight into yourselves, which means an insight into the Chinese way of thinking, Chinese culture and Chinese habits. And actually, everything I know about China, I know from you. We are starting a new era in our lives now, and I wish all of us the best for this era. May we all make the best of our potential. May we all use the good education which we got, especially at the CESL. And may we not forget to be happy, because success I think is not satisfying without happiness in life. I know all of us have huge responsibilities, for our families, for our friends, not least for ourselves; but I am sure we all have the strength to find our way. I will keep you and the time we spent together in my heart, and I hope we will keep in touch and see each other again from time to time, either here in China, which is really my second home now, or in Europe, where you are always welcome.

Thank you.
