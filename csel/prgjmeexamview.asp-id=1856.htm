2011-2012学年欧洲-国际法选修单元D补考试题
发布于  2012/6/28 14:36:42
选修单元D补考试题：“国际贸易”(International Trade)

Title of Course: Intellectual Property Law
Name of Lecturer: Prof. Dr. Marie-Christine Janssens
Instructions:
The students are allowed to use the legal text book that was provided for the course (not the handouts of the slides!!)

Question 1 (theoretical)
A. What is the meaning and significance of the notion ‘acquis communautaire’. Answer this question with reference to legislation in the EU in the sector of copyright law
B. What is the meaning and significance of the ‘right of priority’ in article 4 of the Paris Convention (please do not copy the text!)

Question 2 (practical)
Your client, Digi, is the proprietor of a UK trade mark consisting of a stylised version of the word 'Digi' for 'microcomputer hardware and microcomputer software programs for use in communications between digital electronic devices for use by telecom operators' (see picture).


It comes to the attention of your client that a company named ‘Digipos’ has applied to register as a community trademark the sign DIGIPOS as figurative mark (see picture below) for 'computer hardware, computers, servers, point-of-sale terminals, communications and networking systems, printers, computer software for use in the retail industry'.

  
The application was published in the Community Trade Marks Bulletin of 2 February 2012.             
Your client maintains that on account of the similarity of the respective marks, action should be taken against this CTM application. You are asked to investigate possible ways and grounds for action(s) that Digi could take.
(provide your reasoned decision taking into account the case-law of the Court of Justice; do also anticipate, and evaluate, the arguments DigiPos may raise as a defence).


Title of Course: Transport Law & Insurance
Name of Lecturer: Prof. Marc A. Huybrechts
Instructions: the student should select two questions out of the four questions submitted.

Question:

One: Describe the difference between a Charter Party and the Bill of Lading. Give the three main types of existing charter parties?

Two: What are the three functions of a Bill of Lading in international trade?

Three: more and more Bills of Lading are being replaced by other documents such as a Non Negotiable Seaway Bill (NNSWB).  What is the difference between the Bill of Lading and the Non Negotiable Seaway Bill?  What are some of the advantages of a Non Negotiable Seaway Bill?

Four: In what circumstances would a carrier lose the right or the benefit of limitation of liability under the Hague Visby rules?


Title of Course: International Competiton Law
Name of Lecturer: Prof. Niels Philipsen
Instructions: Students may use language dictionaries as well as the document called ‘legislation and regulatory materials’, which was supplied to them during the course. They may not use slides, law dictionaries or notes.

Question 1
a) What is leniency? Why has it been introduced? (3 p)
b) What are ‘loyalty rebates’? Are they prohibited by EU competition law? (3 points)
c) According to economic theory (and according to the European Commission), horizontal mergers are more likely to negatively affect competition than vertical mergers. Explain why this is so. (4 points)

Question 2
Alfania, Betafina, Gammafina and Deltafina are producers of dog food. Alfania has a market share of 6% on the EU market for dog food; Betafina has a market share of 12% on that market, Gammafina of 8% and Deltafina of 13%. All companies have their main place of business in France. During the previous years they had the following community turnovers: Alfania 12 million euro, Betafina 24 million euro, Gammafina 16 million euro, and Deltafina 25 million euro.
The CEO’s of these four companies met at a conference in Shanghai on the influence of vitamins in pet food on the life span of pets. The four CEO’s complained that their sales figures are decreasing, since as a result of the economic crisis people are buying fewer pets. To keep up their annual turnover, they would need to increase prices.
They agree that with intervals of 3 months they will increase their prices by 2.5%, until prices are 10% higher than they are at present. Alfania, Betafina and Gammafina carry out this plan. Deltafina does not. Is their agreement prohibited by EU law? Please in your answer provide all the (legal) steps necessary to arrive at your conclusion.
