2012-2013学年欧洲-国际法选修单元B考试试题
发布于  2013/3/15 17:09:05
选修单元B：“欧洲行政法”(European Administrative Law)

Title of Course: Administrative Procedures in Competition Matters
Name of Lecturer: Mr Stefan Erich Weishaar
Instructions: Please answer both questions. You are allowed to use the legislation provided to you in the module.

Question 1:
Describe, on the basis of the lectures, in which areas the current application of the procedural rules of EU Competition law - as interpreted by the Commission and the European Court of Justice - may be in conflict with human rights. Give examples.

Model Answer:
Both the European Commission and the Court of Justice of the EU hold that obliging undertakings to deliver factual evidence during an inspection does not militate against the right to remain silent that is protected by Article 6 ECHR. The Court distinguishes between the right to compel to provide factual information and the right to compel admittance of a breach (Orkem). The Court in Strasbourg may have a different opinion on this.
Similarly the correspondence with the lawyer is protected under Article 8 ECHR while under the current LLP-practices only correspondence with external lawyers is protected. Here too the Court in Strasbourg may hold a different opinion.

Best exam


Question 2:
A friend of yours is the Chief Executive Officer of a company situated in Brussels. The European Commission is in front of his factory gates and demands entry on the basis of a declaration based on Article 20(4) of Regulation 1/2003. Since your friend is not able to reach his high-paid lawyer he asks you for advice if he must grant the investigators access to his premises. Does your friend have the right to delay access of the investigators?
Discuss, and do not forget to mention case law.

Model Answer:
Under Article 20(4) of Regulation 1M2003 admittance of the Commission is mandatory. The Commission cannot force entry but will have to rely upon the cooperation of the National authorities for this. Non-admittance will result in a fine (Hoechst). The Commission will respect the right of legal representation and wait 20-30 Minutes for the arrival of the lawyer. Investigators may, however, enter the premises to ensure that evidence is not destroyed and wait before they commence their search (National Panasonic).

Best exam


Title of Course: Administrative Procedures and Judicial Review
Name of Lecturer: Prof. Ulrich Karpen
Instructions: PLEASE ANSWER BOTH QUESTIONS.

Question 1:
F, a private company, has a chemical factory in the town of C. The required license was given to F before the factory was built in the year 2009. In June 2012 F increased its production by 70 %. Shortly after that, many citizens of C started complaining about breathlessness. The environmental agency of C measured the amount of the harmful Chemical X in the emissions of F’s factory and in F’s neighborhood and found that due to the increase of production the general level of Chemical X in C is at 90 µg/m³ in average.

You are an official in the environmental agency and are required to write a statement what the agency could legally do to reduce the level of pollution caused by F’s factory. Especially, your superior wants you to think of different orders he could issue to F and to state which decision he should finally take.

Fictive legal provision:

The environmental agency may take the necessary measures to ensure an average level of Chemical X below the margin of 60 µg/m³.

Question 2:
a. What are the legal consequences of the unlawfulness of an administrative act?
b. Describe the possible reactions of the administration and the concerned citizen in case of an unlawful administrative act!
c. What can the concerned person do in case the illegal administrative act has caused an economic damage to him?


Title of Course: Public Enterprises 
Name of Lecturer: Prof. Jarleth Burke
Instructions: PLEASE ANSWER BOTH QUESTIONS.

Question 1
The Government of Belgium only permits two providers of lottery services in the country. Big Win and Good Fortune are allowed to run a draw and sell tickets under a 1990 Decree law.

Big Win and Good Fortune were awarded licences to provide lottery services in 1995 and 2000 respectively for a period of 30 years. Big Win is a State controlled company and Good Fortune is owned by private investors based in Macau. Big Win and Good Fortune do not compete with each other very vigorously and they have a 50/50 market share.

The Government of Belgium does not have a standard procedure for awarding these licences. It simply reviews applications that are made to it from time to time and makes a decision on a purely discretionary basis.

Big Win and Good Fortune are subject to certain obligations.  Under their licences, they are obliged to make sure that lottery tickets are available to purchase through out the national territory of Belgium, although this requirement is not strictly enforced. In practice both companies have almost complete freedom to decide where their lottery tickets are sold. The price of lottery tickets is not regulated or controlled in any way and both companies are free to decline to sell tickets to particular consumers.

You have been hired as a lawyer in Belgium by Las Vegas Lotteries who have established a very successful lottery ticket business in Germany through a local subsidiary and they wish to establish a branch in Belgium. They make an application to the Belgian Government for a lottery licence, which is refused.

The Belgian Government states that although they acknowledge that Las Vegas Lotteries would be a suitable licensee (having the resources, reputation and technical competence), they do not consider that the market could support three operators. In addition, it says that both Big Win and Good Fortune are subject to important public service obligations and that there is a risk that they will not be able to discharge them if they face competition from Las Vegas Lotteries.

You are asked to advise Las Vegas Lotteries on the legal position under Article 106(1) in conjunction with Article 102 (only), and under 106(2) TFEU.

Question 2
In 2012, the new Italian Government decided to create a German language television service for those parts of Italy near the Austrian border where German is spoken.

The Italian Government approaches RAI, the State controlled broadcaster, and requests it to set up a special subsidiary to do so in return for an exclusive concession. No other broadcaster, Italian or European, was invited to take part in the process for the award of the concession. The service was up and running from March 2012.

The nature of new service, called Deutschital, is left to RAI to decide, the only requirement being that all of the programming come with German audio or subtitles. RAI decides that it will just provide commercial programming, which mainly consists of general entertainment, Hollywood Blockbusters, commercial advertising, and the minimum amount of news required under general Italian law on broadcasting.

Deutschital is set up as a subsidiary of RAI and the Italian Government insists that a clause be inserted into its charter providing that a majority of its directors must be Italian, even if the company was to be eventually privatised. Italy considers this necessary to ensure that the television requirements of German speaking Italians are always satisfied by Deutschital.

The Italian Government provides annual financial assistance for Deutschital to RAI based on what RAI says are the operating costs of the service at the end of the year. The first payment was made in January 2013. There are no criteria specified in advance by Italy as to what costs are recoverable. Fininvest (controlled by Silvio Berlusconi) complains that RAI is highly inefficient compared to its various television services and that the annual payments are and will be a disguised form of State aid to RAI. No State aid notification has been made.

You are a member of the European Commission’s Legal Service and you are asked the following questions following a complaint by Fininvest:

1. Was it lawful for Italy to grant a concession to RAI in the way that it did?
2. Do the payments to RAI for 2012 amount to State Aid, or do they satisfy the Altmark criteria?
3. Can Article 106(2) be applied here and how?
4. Is it lawful for Italy to require that Italian nationals always comprise a majority on the Board of Deutschital?
