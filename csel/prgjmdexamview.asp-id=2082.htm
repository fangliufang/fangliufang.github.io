2012-2013学年欧洲-国际法第三单元考试试题
发布于  2012/12/21 16:47:19
第三单元: 国际公法概论 Module III: Public International Law

Title of Course: Legal Foundations of Public International Law
Name of Lecturer: Prof. Rosemary Byrne
Instructions: Please answer each of the following two questions and Students are permitted to bring an UNMARKED copy of the UN Charter into the examination hall.

Question:

1. Please explain how the way that international law is made through treaty and customary international law reflects the nature and needs of the international legal system.

2. State A has been fighting a rebellion within its borders for a number of years. In 2008 rebel insurgencies increased, supported by financing, training and weapons from State B. State A, which was part of a defensive alliance with States C and D requested assistance with dealing with the increasing rebel insurgencies. States C and D carried out a number of military actions against the rebels in State A.

Throughout 2009 and 2010, however, the rebels made significant gains, and by August 2010 controlled around half of the territory and population. On 1st January 2011 the rebels gained control of the capital of State A, and the then government fled to State C.

Shortly afterwards the rebels declared themselves the legitimate government of State A. Nonetheless, States C and D continued to mount operations in State A with the stated objective of restoring the previous Government of State A. In the course of these attacks a large number of civilians were killed in State A.
State B, which was an ally of the new government in State A, began fighting with the troops of States C and D within State A, claiming to act in furtherance of a collective right to self-defence. Although State B referred the situation to the Security Council, the Security council was unable to agree to appropriate action due to a veto by one of the Permanent 5 Members of the Security Council

Discuss the legality of under international law of the actions of:
a) State B; and
b) States C and D.


Title of Course: International Organization
Name of Lecturer: Mr Colm Mac Eochaidh
Instructions: Discuss one only of the following statements, citing practical examples and/or case law where appropriate:

Question:
1. In Tadic, the Appeals Chamber of the International Criminal Tribunal for the Former Yugoslavia approached the issue of powers in a similar way to the International Court of Justice.             

2. While few doubt the need for reform of the UN Security Council, the P5 veto, the politics of the General Assembly and regional rivalries make reform very difficult to achieve.


Title of Course: China in International Law
Name of Lecturer: Mr David Conlan Smyth
Instructions: Please answer each of the following two questions

Question:
1. Although china has become a fully integrated member of the international community, it still practices a policy of non-intervention in international affairs. Describe the development of China’s engagement with the outside world in the sphere of public international law.

2. The Hong Kong special administrative region displays characteristics of a “state within a state”. Discuss.
