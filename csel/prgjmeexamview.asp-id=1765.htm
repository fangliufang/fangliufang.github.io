2011-2012学年欧洲-国际法选修单元D考试试题
发布于  2012/6/28 12:56:56
选修单元D：“国际贸易法” (International Trade)
Title of Course: Intellectual Property Law
Name of Lecturer: Prof. Dr. Marie-Christine Janssens
Instructions:
The students are allowed to use the legal text book that was provided for the course (not the handouts of the slides!!)

Question 1 (theoretical)
One of the main objectives of Directive 2001/29 on copyright and related rights in the information society was to complete the harmonisation of the fundamental concepts of copyright law in the EU Member States. 
a. Why is this harmonization process so difficult?
b. Discuss to what extent the EU succeeded to achieve its goal so far?

Question 2 (practical)
Your client, the German company Markant  is the proprietor of a German registration for the word mark CASTELLUCA, dated 20 August, 1997, for ‘wines’, in Class 33.

It comes to the attention of your client that an Italian company named Castellani has applied to register as a community trademark the figurative sign Castellani (see picture below).
 

This application was published in the Community Trade Marks Bulletin of March 28, 2012.             
The goods in respect of which registration is sought are in class 33, and correspond to the following description: ‘alcoholic beverages except beer, liqueur, sparkling wine and Champagne’.

Your client maintains that on account of the similarity of the respective marks, action should be taken against this CTM application.

You are asked to investigate possible ways and grounds for action(s) that your client could take.

(Provide a reasoned decision taking into account the case-law of the Court of Justice; do also anticipate, and assess, the arguments the applicant (i.e. Italian Company)  could raise as a defense).


Title of Course: Transport Law & Insurance
Name of Lecturer: Prof. Marc A. Huybrechts
Instructions: the each student should select two questions he/she wants to answer out of the six questions submitted.

Please note that question A one and A two, B one and B two, C one and C two, D one and D two, E one and E two and F one and F two are to be considered as “one question” to be answered.  Don't split these up!

Question:
A1: What is the difference between global/tonnage limitation in favour of the vessel owner and the package limitation in favour of the carrier in transport law?
A2: Why has the legislator given the benefit of limitation of liability to a maritime carrier or an overland carrier for claims the carrier owes to victims and contracting parties with whom he dealt? Why is the victim not fully compensated?

B1: What is the difference between the Uniform system and the Network system in multimodal transport?
B2 : Discuss a serious drawback in the Network system.

C1: What is the limitation amount per kilo ( or per unit or package ) in the following transport treaties:
One: CMR 1956
Two: Hague Visby Rules (per package /unit and per kilo)
Three: Montréal convention 1999(air law)
Four: the Rotterdam Rules (per package/unit and per kilo)
Five: the UN Convention on Multimodal Transport 1980(per package and per kilo)

C2:The Limitation amount is ( 3 SDR) but how much is that in your local/national currency? How many Yuan ? Where should you look to find the value in your own national currency of one special drawing right (SDR)?

D1: Describe the difference between a carrier (the real carrier or the contractual carrier) and the Freight Forwarder or an Ocean Freight forwarder.
D2: What is a Multimodal Transport Operator?

E1: What is meant by the “ nautical fault exemption” in maritime transport law?
E2:Is the nautical fault exemption available to the carrier in the Rotterdam Rules ?

F1: What is the difference between a charter-party and a transport contract covered by a traditional Bill of Lading?
F2: By what International Treaty are Charter Parties regulated?


Title of Course: International Competiton Law
Name of Lecturer: Prof. Niels Philipsen
Instructions: Students may use language dictionaries as well as the document called ‘legislation and regulatory materials’, which was supplied to them during the course. They may not use slides, law dictionaries or notes.

Question:
Question 1
a) Explain the concept of ‘relevant market’ and its importance in EU competition law. (3 points)
b) What is ‘tying’? Is it prohibited by EU competition law? (3 points)
c) What is the Herfindahl-Hirschman index? (2 points)
d) What are dawn raids? (2 points)

Question 2
The region of Upper Land wants to ensure the operation of an emergency ambulance service in its region. The regional regulation entrusts General Ambulance Service with the task of providing a permanent standby service of transporting sick and injured persons in emergencies throughout the region, at uniform rates and on similar quality conditions, without regard to the particular situations or to the degree of economic profitability of the individual transport operation. Providing these services is not very profitable, however. To maintain a 24 hour emergency ambulance service throughout the region, including more remote areas, requires costly investment in equipment and qualified personnel. The regional authority therefore grants General Ambulance Service an exclusive right over the provision of another, profitable service: non-emergency transport. With the profits it can raise from the non-emergency transport services, it is able to off-set the inevitable losses it would suffer from providing the emergency transport services.

Quality Ambulance Services is a private company that sees an opportunity to obtain economic advantage by competing with General Ambulance Service for the provision of non-emergency transport service. It challenges General Ambulance Service’s exclusive right to provide non-emergency transport services. Is this challenge likely to be successful on the basis of EU competition rules and practice?
