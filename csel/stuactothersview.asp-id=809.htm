A social event between CESL's professors and students
发布于  2010/9/9 11:08:59
On 24th October 2008, CESL held a gathering for the professors and students to have an exchange. The event began at 6:00pm with the introduction of the professors to the students. Prof. Colneric was first to introduce herself and the others professors followed suit. After the introduction, refreshment and snacks were served, and all the attendees started to exchange their thoughts on various issues.

The discussions were vivid on the floor. I started talking with a professor from Autonoma University, Madrid. We began by talking about the difficulties that the Chinese students are facing in their courses, and that they might more need time to get used to the formats of teaching. The professor agreed that there will be some difficulties as lots of courses are being taught in such a short timeframe, yet he was sure that the Chinese students will go fine, as they are all very intelligent as assured by his colleagues. He asked for any suggestions for improvement, and I replied that Chinese students are eager to have more discussions during classes. He believed that it is a good idea, and will suggest it to the board.

We then moved on to the topic of course contents. I suggested that a course on Western political thoughts should be offered as political ideas are the basis on legal development. The professor was astonished by the suggestion and he agreed with me wholeheartedly, as he had also studied political science himself. He told me that there are law professors who do not agree with such position and they view law itself as a strictly scientific discipline, and that it should be discussed as the law. He suggested me to open up discussion on the issues with other professors during my time at CESL and to see if they can be convinced.

After the productive discussion, I then moved to talk with a professor from University of Manchester. The professor told me that he is an economist in charging of the school of law back in Manchester, and it is very important for legal students to understand other disciplines. Other students told him about their difficulties and he suggested that we should have a rule that says students should be speaking in English at all time, which would allow the improvement of English in a rather short time. I said we will try to enforce the rule to our capacity, and he was fast to comment that law is not law without enforcement. He then assured that the students will do fine in CESL. He was curtained because he had past experience in teaching foreign students and he could see that students at CESL are highly intelligent.

All the attendees were enjoying themselves, and it seemed that all students had benefited by engaging into discussions. Time was passing fast, and before we knew it the professors had to leave for their meals. As a student I look forward to the next opportunity to have conversations with them, and I think all students think the same.

                                                                                                                                       By Alton Cheung
