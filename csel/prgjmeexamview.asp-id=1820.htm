2011-2012学年欧洲-国际法选修单元D二次补考试题
发布于  2012/6/30 8:24:42
选修单元D二次补考试题：“国际贸易”(International Trade)

Title of Course: Intellectual Property Law
Name of Lecturer: Prof. Dr. Marie-Christine Janssens

Instructions:
The students are allowed to use the legal text book that was provided for the course (not the handouts of the slides!!)

Question 1

Above, you see the picture of a new design chair that company X intends to introduce on the market in September 2012. Before doing so, it seeks your advice as to possible protection that could be relied on or obtained under the system of intellectual property rights.

Please provide company X with your reasoned advice (indicating also what the advantages/disadvantages of specific choices could be).

Question 2
A French engineer designed a telecommunications tower.  After the tower was built in Paris, hairline cracks appeared, causing the owner to fear that the tower might collapse.  The owner modified the external structure of the tower to reinforce it.  The engineer sued the owner for breach of his copyright, including his moral rights. 

2.a. Discuss the relevant legal issues, and possible arguments of the two parties
2.b. Would your analysis be different if the tower had been built by an US engineer in the US and, if so, explain why.


Title of Course: Transport Law & Insurance
Name of Lecturer: Prof. Marc A. Huybrechts
Instructions: the student should select two questions out of the four questions submitted.

Question:
1) What is a non negotiable sea waybill( NNSW) and in what way is it different from a traditional bill of lading (B/L)?

2) The Hague ?CVisby Rules constitute a “big” compromise between ocean carriers on the one side and shippers/consignees on the other side: describe the main features of this compromise (What is in for the carriers? What is in for the consignees and Shippers?)

3) Suppose a consignment of goods is discharged out of the vessel “M/V Idle Hope” after an ocean carriage from Hong Kong to Hamburg in Germany. The Hague Visby Rules are applicable to this Bill of Lading. However the goods are seriously damaged and some goods are short landed. The consignee files a “protest” against the carrier when taking delivery of the goods. What is the consequence for the carrier by filing such a protest? Suppose that the consignee failed to file a timely protest in this case. What would have been the consequences for him?

4) In what circumstances would an ocean carrier lose the benefit of the package limitation liability under the Hague Visby Rules and under the Rotterdam Rules?


Title of Course: International Competiton Law
Name of Lecturer: Prof. Niels Philipsen
Instructions: Students may use language dictionaries as well as the document called ‘legislation and regulatory materials’, which was supplied to them during the course. They may not use slides, law dictionaries or notes.

Question 1
a) How is the concept of ‘undertaking’ defined in EU competition law? (2 points)
b) Why, according to economists, are vertical agreements often not likely to negatively effect competition? In your answer, also mention some positive effects that vertical agreements may have on competition. (3 points)
c) How can the relevant product market be determined in EU competition law? (3 points)
d) What is predatory pricing? (2 points)

Question 2
The region of Upper Land wants to ensure the operation of an emergency ambulance service in its region. The regional regulation entrusts General Ambulance Service with the task of providing a permanent standby service of transporting sick and injured persons in emergencies throughout the region, at uniform rates and on similar quality conditions, without regard to the particular situations or to the degree of economic profitability of the individual transport operation. Providing these services is not very profitable, however. To maintain a 24 hour emergency ambulance service throughout the region, including more remote areas, requires costly investment in equipment and qualified personnel. The regional authority therefore grants General Ambulance Service an exclusive right over the provision of another, profitable service: non-emergency transport. With the profits it can raise from the non-emergency transport services, it is able to off-set the inevitable losses it would suffer from providing the emergency transport services.

Quality Ambulance Services is a private company that sees an opportunity to obtain economic advantage by competing with General Ambulance Service for the provision of non-emergency transport service. It challenges General Ambulance Service’s exclusive right to provide non-emergency transport services. Is this challenge likely to be successful on the basis of EU competition rules and practice?
