2011-2012学年欧洲-国际法选修单元E考试试题
发布于  2012/6/28 13:04:47
选修单元E：“公司法” (Company Law)

Title of Course: Legal Business Forms & Groups of Companies
Name of Lecturer: Prof. Stefan Rammeloo

Question:
Mr. X is a Chinese entrepreneur, planning to set up an undertaking in Europe with a view to trade in Chinese sweets (Tanghulurs). The idea is to incorporate business either as German private limited company, or as a German UG.

1 Does it make a difference whether, in particular in view of capital requirements, a GmbH or an UG is set up? Please take into account both German law and European Union Law.

2 Suppose that after a certain period Mr. X wishes to relocate his enterprise to France without however changing the German company structure. May France raise objections to this plan, from European Union law point of view?

Reference answer:
Answer 1 (6 credits, 3 credits per ‘indent’):
- Capital requirement German Law: GmbH/UG: (Power Point slide 50) ?25.000-; UG: ?1
- Capital requirement EU Law: none, as 2nd Company Law Directive applies to public limited liability companies only

Answer 2 (4 credits):
- Negative (PP slide 116), under articles 43 and 48 (currently: 49 and 54 TfEU) inbound migration of company’s headquarters is established by CJEU in Inspire Art Ltd.



Title of Course: Corporate Finance
Name of Lecturer: Prof. Rainer Kulms

Question:

Question 1: A car producer approaches a bank for a credit of 1m Euros. Please advise the bank on how to best protect its interests in being repaid. In preparing your answer, please discuss the essentials of a pledge agreement and the business interests of the parties to such an agreement.

Question 2: The board of the C corporation is approached by the board of the X corporation to enter into negotiations on the acquisition of the C shares by X. What are the duties of the board of directors of both corporations? Explain the various steps to be taken from the very beginning of negotiations until the realization of an acquisition agreement.


Title of Course: Corporate Governance
Name of Lecturer: Prof. Stefan Messmann

Question:
Question 1)
Recently, I visited several Chinese companies and asked the general managers of these companies what do they think about the introduction of the German workers co-determination model into Chinese companies. The answers I received were quite clear:” The Emperor is the Emperor and the peasants are the peasants" - I was told.
Nevertheless, please give your opinion of the opportunity and usefulness of the introduction of the German workers co-determination model into Chinese
- state-owned enterprises,
- private enterprises,
- Chinese-foreign joint enterprises (joint ventures), and
- foreign wholly-owned companies.

Question 2)
The EU Commission lanced some years ago a public consultation on the limitation of auditors' liability. The Commission asked the opinions for altering auditors' liability. The stakeholders should choose among the following four (4) options:
a) the introduction of a fixed monetary cap,
b) the introduction of a cap based on the size of the audited company,
c) he introduction of a cap based on a multiple of the audit fees charges by the auditor to its clients or
d) the introduction by the Member States of the EU of the principle of proportionate liability, which means that each party (auditor and audited company) is liable only for the proportion of loss that corresponds to the party's degree of responsibility.
What would be your choice and WHY?
