本院学生代表队参加上海世博会Model-EU活动闭幕典礼
发布于  2010/9/9 10:09:30
2010年5月12日，本院学生代表队参加了在上海世博园欧盟展馆举行的Model-EU活动闭幕典礼。以下为本院2009级双硕士学生李冉从上海发回的英文报道：

After two days of heated debate, the Model EU came to a close on May 12, 2010. Student delegates from the European Commission, the European Parliament, the European Council, stakeholders and the media each presented their conclusions at the afternoon session, which was presided over by Franz Jessen, Director General External Relations of the European Commission.

Decision making in the EU is a complicated process. Over the two days the Commission first presented legislation to Parliament and the EU Council. Whilst Council accepted the original proposals, the European Parliament did not, stating that the car industry had failed to comply with a voluntary agreement signed in 1998. Amendments were made whereby the entire cost of new legislation should be met by the car industry. Parliament ended the integrated approach, decreased limits on CO2 emissions, and reduced the time for implementation. Legislation could now only be approved if Council accepted the amendments. If Council does not accept the amendment they must establish a "common position", which must then be approved by Parliament.

The EU Council is composed of ministers from the 27 member states, each of whom have both shared and competing interests. Third year law student, He Xin and first year law student, Li Ran, were assigned to the Council group as ministers for Lithuania and Luxembourg respectively. Students in this group were required to act in the interest of their new country. Luxembourg and Lithuania to some extent reflect the diversity of the EU. The two countries have totally different levels of GDP, opposing positions on the reduction of CO2 emissions, and also belong to different interest groups.

Luxembourg, a small but wealthy country in Western Europe, only has four votes on the council, (the number of votes is limited according to population numbers). Thus in order to achieve Luxembourg’s desired objectives it was essential that Li Ran joined other countries with the same interests. On the issue of environmental protection she worked with countries such as Denmark and Sweden. However, national priorities also played an important part as Luxembourg, who has a tyre manufacturing industry, needed to co-operate with countries such as Germany and France who have large automotive industries and consume Luxembourg’s products and support an integrated approach. This would mean that costs would be spread across industries, not only met  by the automobile manufacturers, but also by those producing goods such as air conditioners. The Council could not accept all of parliament's proposals and thus a "common position"was compiled bringing together the various opinions. Following which CESL student Li Bo presented the Environment Committee’s amended proposal and made comments on the “common position" advanced by the Council.

CESL student Wu Jing expressed her thanks to the organizers, in the section on experience-sharing and feedback. She also talked of how the Model-EU was helpful in allowing students to gain a better understanding of the EU’s decision making procedures. Students learned about how legislation is proposed, debated and amended by various parties from officials who work at the EU Commission, Parliament and Secretariat. The Model-EU also gave students a chance to practice negotiation strategies and improve their English language skills, learning a spirit of co-operation and the need to balance competing interests.
本院2009级双硕士学生李博在发言
